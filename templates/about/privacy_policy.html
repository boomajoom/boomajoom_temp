In a form that doesn't suck.

An email address collected as a result of signing up with Boomajoom.com (known as "the site" or "the service") or contacting the site will be used to help in your use of the site and communication relating to your inquiry. It doesn't mean that the site will use that email address to try to sell you something.

Self explanatory.

The only potential caveat to this is if you contact an advertiser or partner of Boomajoom using a web property or advertisement published by Boomajoom. In this case, information you submit will be transmitted to the advertiser. If you dial a telephone call forwarding number provided by Boomajoom from a landline, your caller ID information may be transmitted to the advertiser.

Full data destruction online is nearly impossible. Depending on your selected provider and the provider you are contacting, emails may be recovered years after they are deleted. However, the site itself does not save anything you send to us for longer than a month (unless you are a paying customer and these records are relevant to your account. The only potential exception is with the use of some kind of digital forensics program, which brings us to the last point...

This site is based in California, and thus must follow the legal procedures of that state as well as the United States. Legal warrants signed by a judge must be presented before any information from the site is provided to law enforcement.
